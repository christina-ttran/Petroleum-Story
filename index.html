How Oily Are You?

We often think of oil as something we pump into cars or see in headlines about energy crises. But petroleum is woven into the fabric of our daily lives — hidden in the clothes we wear, the food we eat, the phones we use, and the way we move through the world. This interactive experience walks you through a typical day and invites you to explore how much oil it really takes to live modern life. Select your routine, and we’ll calculate the unseen energy behind it.

Food

From farm to fork, oil is everywhere. It fuels tractors, powers processing plants, and wraps your lunch in plastic. Even a simple meal carries a hidden energy cost — one most people never taste.

Transportation

Whether it’s a solo drive to school or a flight across the country, transportation is one of the most oil-hungry parts of daily life. Gasoline, diesel, and jet fuel are all derived from petroleum — and every mile traveled burns through more than just time. How far did your day take you?

Clothing

We don’t often think of fashion as fuel, but the clothes we wear are stitched together with oil. From synthetic fibers like polyester to global shipping and factory energy, petroleum powers nearly every stage of the fashion industry. What we wear comes at an invisible cost.

Technology
